There are 6 cases in Russian language - Nominative, Genitive, Dative, Accusative, Instrumental, and Prepositional.
Personal Pronouns
In addition to cases, pronouns are identified by three persons and can be singular or plural.
-First person pronouns refer to the speaker (Я, МЫ)
-Second person pronouns refer to the person you are talking to (ТЫ, ВЫ)
-Third person pronouns refer to the person or an object you are speaking about (ОН, ОНА, ОНО, ОНИ)

4. Accusative - indicates direct object of an action or feeling. Mary doesn't like him. Blame whom? (Винить кого?)

6. Prepositional - always used with preposition. Bob mentioned about her. Thinking of whom? (О ком?)

About him, About her, About them

English pronoun "thou" is used very seldom, however in Russian language "Ты" is very common and is used mostly for friends, close family members, and younger people or children.


Possesive Pronouns - indicate who something belongs to. A helpful question to use - "whose?" ("Чей? Чья? Чьё?") Мой Твой Наш Ваш Его Её Их
Reflexive Pronouns - indicate "self" and is used when the pronoun is the same person as the subject Сам, Сама, Себя, Свой, Своя Каждый Любой Какой-нибудь Чей-нибудь
Demonstrative Pronouns - points to the subject of a conversation Этот Тот Такой Столько


Types of Pronouns Uses Pronouns Examples
1. Possesive Indicates who something belongs to Мой (my), Твой (your), Наш (our), Ваш (your), Его (his), Их (their), Свой (my/your)
Моя (my), Твоя (your), Наша (our), Ваша (your), Её (her), Своя (my/your)
 Мой дом. (My house), Его книга. (His book), Её ребёнок. (Her child), Их машина. (Their car)
2. Reflexive Indicate "self" and is used when the pronoun is the same person as the subject Сам (myself/himself), Себя (self), Самому (myself/yourself), Сами (ourselves/themselves)
Сама (myself), Самой (myself), Себя (self)
Он любит себя. (He loves himself), Она сделала себе чай. (She made tea for herself), Я сделаю это сама. (I will do it myself)
3. Interrogative Ask questions Кто? (who?), Что? (what?), Какой? (what/which?), Который? (which?), Чей? (whose?), Сколько? (how many?)
Какая? (what/which?), Которая? (which?), Чья? (whose?)
Кто там? (Who is there?), Какой твой любимый цвет? (What is your favorite color?), Который час? (What time is it?), Чей это телефон? (Whose phone is it?), Сколько это стоит? (How much is it?)
4. Relative Same as interrogative pronouns, but used for connecting parts of a sentence Кто (who), Что (what), Какой (what/which), Который (which), Чей (whose), Сколько (how many)
Какая (what/which), Которая (which), Чья (whose)
 Это то, что они кушают. (This is what they eat), Какая хорошая песня. (What a good song)
5. Negative Same as interrogative pronouns, but are used to describe the absence of subjects, things, people. Add "ни" or "не" to the word. Никто (nobody), Ничто (nothing), Никакой (neither), Ничей (nobody's), Нисколько (nothing/none)
Никакая (neither), Ничья (nobody's) 		Никто не пришёл на урок. (Nobody came to the lesson), Это нисколько не стоит. (It costs nothing), Это ничья сумка. (This is nobody's bag)
6. Indefinite Same as interrogative pronouns, but are used to describe indefinite subject or quality. Add "не" or "кое" to the beginning of the word or "-то", "-либо", "-нибудь" to the end. Некто (someone), Нечто (something) Кто-то (someone), Что-то (something), Кто-либо (somebody), Что-либо (anything), Какой-либо (any), Какой-нибудь (any/some), Чей-то (someone's), Кое-что (something), Кое-кто (someone)
Какая-нибудь (any), Какая-либо (any), Чья-то (someone's), Чья-либо (someone's) 		Выбери какой-нибудь цвет. (Pick any color), Что-то упало. (Something fell down), Чья-то книга на столе. (Someone's book is on the table)
7. Demonstrative Points to the subject of a conversation Это (this), Этот (this), Тот (that), Такой (such), Столько (so many)
Эта (this), Та (that), Такая (such) 		Это книга. (This is a book), То яблоко жёлтое. (That apple is yellow), Такой теплый день. (Such a warm day), Здесь столько цветов. (There are so many flowers here)
8. Determinative Indicate certain, but not specific object or a person Самый (most), Всякий (any), Весь (all), Каждый (every), Любой (any)
Самая (most), Всякая (any), Вся (all), Каждая (every), Любая (any) 		Это самая интересная история. (This is the most interesting story), Он бежал весь путь. (He ran all the way), Выбери любую картину. (Choose any painting)
