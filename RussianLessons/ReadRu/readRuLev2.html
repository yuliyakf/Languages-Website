As you begin reading you will notice that some Russian letters are written in one way but sound in a different way.

For example: when letter "О" is in a weak position (not stressed) in a word it sounds like "А"

КосА [а], ОсА [а], МолокО [а]

Strong consonants at the end of a word or if placed before a dull consonant ( "П" "Ф" "К" "Т" "Ш" "С" ) will make a weak sound

Вторник [f], Дуб [p], Глаз [s] , Зуб [p]

Letter "Ч" pronounced as "Ш" in a few words

Что [sh], Конечно [sh]

A combination of letters "ЖЧ" "ЗЧ" "СЧ" pronounced as "Щ"

Мужчина [soft sh], Заказчик [soft sh], Счёт [soft sh]


Uppercase letters are used to show stressed sounds. If it helps try to sing them.

Letters "Ч" "Щ" are always soft. When paired with letters "К" "Н" "Р" "Т" never use "ь" soft sign to soften it.

"ЧК" "ЧН" "НЧ" "ЧТ" "РЩ" "ЩН" "НЩ"

Мечта, Дочка, Бабочка, Каменщик, Женщина, Скворечник, Птенчик

Some adjectives in singular form that have ending "-ого" "-его" pronounce "-ово" "-ево" instead of "г". Likewise, the word "Сегодня" translates "today", pronounces as "Севодня"

Большого, Хорошего, Моего, Красного, Красивого, Одного, Сегодня
